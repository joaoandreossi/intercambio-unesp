Arriving in Brazil


After your plane lands you will find yourself in one of three possible airports in the state of São Paulo. Be sure to know which one by checking your boarding pass.


São Paulo International Airport – Cumbica (GRU)

Rodovia Hélio Smidt, Cumbica - Guarulhos, 07190-100, Brasil
Telephone number: 55 11 2445 2945 – 24 hours a day
Website: www.aeroportoguarulhos.net


Viracopos International Airport (VCP)

Rodovia Santos Dumont, Km 66 – Campinas –São Paulo
Website: www.viracopos.com


Congonhas São Paulo Airport (CGH)

Avenida Washington Luiz, s/nº - São Paulo
Website: www.aeroportocongonhas.net



The next step is to get to a bus terminal that offer connections to Bauru. If you're arriving from Viracopos you need to get to Rodoviaria de Campinas (Campinas Bus Station), follow this link for more information on the best way to get there. After you arrive at the bus station, search for the Expresso de Prata booth and ask for a ticket to Bauru, you can find prices and timetables for buses on this link.


If you're arriving at Congonhas or Cumbica, you will need to get to Barra Funda Bus Station. The easiest way is using the Airport Bus Service, you can find more information on this page. Other options include Uber and the city transport, however the latter might be a bit difficult for students that do not speak Portuguese or those carrying a lot of luggage.

At Barra Funda you can search for either Expresso de Prata or Reunidas Paulista booth for a ticket, you can use the BuscaOnibus website to check price and times.


IMPORTANT: While bus services do allow foreigners to purchase bus tickets with the proper identification, some may require Brazilian identification (CPF) to purchase them online, so you will have to buy your tickets on the company booth located at the bus terminal. You MUST have either your passport with you at the time of boarding to take buses between cities. Arriving without the proper identification can cause you to be unable to board the bus.


Arriving in Bauru

You are going to arrive at Bauru Bus Station. Depending on the time of day, you may want to head to the UNESP campus and introduce yourself to the Escritorio de Relações Internacionais (Office of International Relations). They will help you with local information, to find you a place to stay and to get national documentation.

The easiest way to get there is to use a Uber or a taxi, though taxis can be expensive as the campus is far from the bus terminal.

Another option is get a city bus, just wait at the bus stop across the street for the bus 4832 - Isaura P. Garmes - Unesp/ CTI, the campus is the second to last stop, but keep in mind that without speaking Portuguese and with a lot of luggage it can be difficult.

At the campus you can ask other students or the people at the gates for directions to the Escritorio de Relações Internacionais of the Faculdade de Ciencias (Faculty of Science, or FC).


When you arrive at the office introduce yourself, then you will be asked to fill a form with your personal information. You will need to present the following documents.

i. passport (with visa);
ii. travel insurance;
iii. acceptance letter;
iv. declaration of financial support (it may be the same used for the visa application at the Brazilian embassy or consulate).


Obtaining a Registro Nacional de Estrangeiros (RNE)

You will also need to go to the Brazilian Federal Police within 30 days to register, which will guarantee a legal stay in Brazil. A fine will be charged for every day of delay.

The temporary stay document for foreigners in Brazil is called “Registro Nacional de Estrangeiros” (RNE). To obtain this document you will need to go to a Policia Federal (Federal Police) office, you can start the process and schedule a visit by following the instructions in this link.

The address for the Federal Police offices in Bauru is Av. Getúlio Vargas, 20-55 - Jardim Europa, Bauru - SP, 17017-383. You will need to be there on the date chosen during your online registration to continue with your application. Bring with you the following documents;

i. filled out form on Federal Police website, after filling out the form online, print it;
ii. two 3cm x 4cm recent photos with white background and no date;
iii. original passport and notary-certified copy of each used page (look for a “cartório”);
iv. original visa application;
v. consular inscription certificate, in Portuguese, that must be obtained in Brazilian consulate in your country of origin;
vi. notary-certified copy of your entry/departure card;
vii. original payment receipt of the two taxes required.


To generate your bank receipt, in order to pay the R$125 fee to emit the RNE, access www.dpf.gov.br and follow the instructions below.

Select the option: Serviços > GRU > GRU-FUNAPOL > third option for foreign individuals (pessoas estrangeiras). Fill out the form twice. The first time, at “unidade arrecadadora” select SP (027-2) SUPERINTENDENCIA REGIONAL NO ESTADO DE SÃO PAULO”. In “código de reserva” select the code “140082 – Registro de estrangeiro”. Click on “gerar guia” and print.

The second time, in “unidade arrecadadora” selec the option “SP (027-2) SUPERINTENDENCIA REGIONAL NO ESTADO DE SÃO PAULO”. In the field “Código de Receita”, choose the code “código 140120 – Carteira de Estrangeiro de 1ª via” this time. Again, select “gerar guia” and print. Pay both of the bank receipts at any bank.

All the listed documentation must be presented at the Federal Police. The institution will give you a protocol with your RNE number. If the application confirmation document does not have a RNE number, ask for a number immediately.
